Math Question Understanding and Answering Dataset

This database contains curated sets of K-12 math curriculum based "psets" for training neural nets and bots for automated explanation of strategies for solving math problems. The problems in the PSETS contain detailed step by step strategies for solving a given problem. The PSETS will also be correlated to a detailed concept map that captures the relationships among topics in the entire K-12 math curriculum.

The primary aim of the PSETS is to serve as a train set for a new family of deep neural nets focused on inductive reasoning . The step by step solution and associated meta-data of problem solving strategies are aimed at enhancing the inductive reasoning capabilities of a neural net. The aim of the training is not on providing the right answer, but also on explaning how to solve the given problem. Can the automated system learn to generalize the explaination for each step involved in the solution to a given math problem?

In addition to curriculum oriented problems, the PSETS contain problems from following math competitions and olympiad. These problems and step by step strategies for their solution are included to help train an automated bot to compete at the highest levels of math competitions.
  • Mathematical Olympiad for Elementary and Middle Schools (MOEMS)
  • Mathcounts
  • AMC 8 / 10 / 12 and AIME Competitions
  • American Regions Mathematics League (ARML)
  • International Mathematical Olympiad
  • William Lowell Putnam Mathematical Competition

We will also utilize problems from past olympiad competitions (from all over the world).
Out ultimate goal is to build an automated bot that can one day compete in and win the International Math Olymiad (IMO)! While winning the IMO be a nice icing on the cake, our real aim is to build several bots that help kids learn, enjoy, and appreciate the beauty of math!
