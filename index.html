Math Question Understanding and Answering Dataset


Recently a group of researchers at Deep Mind (Google) have demonstrated the feasibility of an automated system's ability to solve and explain algebraic word problems. Inspired by this work, we are working on a curated sets of mathematical problem for the training of deep neural nets.

This dataset contains curated sets of K-12 curriculum based "psets" for training neural nets and bots for automated explanation of strategies for solving math problems. The problems in the PSETS contain detailed step by step strategies for solving the given problem.
The primary focus of the PSETS is to train a neural net. The aim of the training is not on providing the right answer, but also on explaning how to solve the given problem. Can the automated system explain each step involved in the solution to the problem?
The use of the curriculm map enables the automated system to detect and monitor the progress of a learner and the system can adjust and adapt its dialog and explanation to cater to the needs of the learner.
In addition to curriculum oriented problems, the PSETS contain problems from following math competitions and olympiad. These problems and step by step strategies for their solution are included to help train an automated bot to compete at the highest levels of math competitions.
  • Mathematical Olympiad for Elementary and Middle Schools (MOEMS)
  • Mathcounts
  • AMC 8 / 10 / 12 and AIME Competitions
  • American Regions Mathematics League (ARML)
  • International Mathematical Olympiad
  • William Lowell Putnam Mathematical Competition

We will also utilize problems from past olympiad competitions (from all over the world).
Out ultimate goal is to build an automated bot can one day win the IMO! Along the way, we hope to build several bots that help kids learn math!
